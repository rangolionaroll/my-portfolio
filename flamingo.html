Disguise

This piece was created for "Flamingos: A Covert Project." in 2019. I created an original art piece converting a lawn flamingo into a peacock using laser cut acrylic and acrylic paint. It was displayed by Artfront Galleries in Newark.

When I got the flamingo for this exhibit, I was told to do something interesting with it. I thought it would be really cool to turn it into a lawn peacock with a purple color scheme. To give it the headpiece and feathers I drew the shape on a piece of paper with a marker and then put it into Inkscape to create a vector drawing. The shapes were then cut out of an acrylic sheet using a laser cutter.

I used an initial coat of acrylic paint on the body of the flamingo, the feathers and the headpiece. Then, I used an X-Acto knife to cut out small rectangles on the flamingo's body to insert the feathers. The feathers and headpiece had tabs at the bottom of them to hold them in place.

After inserting the headpiece and feathers into the body, I tested them to make sure they stayed in place as seen in the video. While the tabs worked, and I enjoyed the feathers being able to move, I noticed that a lot of the paint was being scraped off by the movement and the headpiece was falling into the head, so I decided to use a bit of hot glue to reinforce them.

Afterwards the details on the flamingo were painted on with acrylic paint and a varnish was applied to keep it protected since it was to be displayed outdoors.
