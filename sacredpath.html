Sacred Path Charms

These charms were created originally for Douglass Residential College's Sacred Path in 2019, and repainted in 2022.

For three of my undergrad years, I served as Ceremony Chair for Douglass Residential College's Traditional Events Committee, and planned two ceremonies Yule Log and Sacred Path. Sacred Path, when it first started in 1919, marked the first time first-year students were allowed to walk the path between College Hall and George Street. While that tradition no longer stands, Sacred Path still marks the moving up of each year, and is celebrated with a charm representing each year.

First-year students receive a lantern to symbolize the knowledge in the world around them; sophomores receive a clock to represent their time at Douglass; juniors are given a key to show passion, energy, and leadership; seniors are given a mortarboard to commemorate their time at Douglass and symbolize their passage through the program; and alumnae are given an ivy charm to show their continued growth with Douglass. In 2019, flowers were presented to Incoming Students as a symbol of vibrancy and growth.

The charms pictured above had been used in the ceremony for longer than most of the committee could remember so I decided that I wanted to remake them to be larger, more vibrant and consistent.

For material, I was given unused particle board that was in the basement of College Hall. I used simple vector art that I found online for each of the charms and then modified them to be svg files for the CNC at the Makerspace. One of the staff members there showed me how to use the CNC and switch out the bits and which bits to use. For three of the charms the depth of my engraving went deeper than I prefered but I still liked the overall look of the charms. Pictured below are the charms before painting.

With little time before the ceremony, I used acrylic paint and painted the charms by hand for the 2019 ceremony. Afterwards, I picked up spray paint, primer, and varnish so that I would be able to give the charms full coverage with the paint and redo some of the details, and also protect the charms from damage in the future.

The pandemic happened and canceled the 2020 ceremony so I wasn't able to return and finish the project until 2022. But am happy to have delivered some charms that will last years like the older ones.
