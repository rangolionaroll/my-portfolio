In Their Shoes: A VPVA Program

This project is a series of 2D games designed for The Office for Violence Prevention and Victim Assistance at Rutgers Newark.

Roles: Game Designer, Artist & Animator, Story Editor, Website Designer and Developer

Introduction


This game was designed as a 2D choose your own adventure game in which participants can step into the shoes of a character who is experiencing interpersonal violence, and navigate through a portion of their story. In order to answer the question "Why don't they just leave?" the players can make decisions as the characters themselves and play through scenarios which show the consequences of the decisions they make.

Immersive Storytelling

    The objective of this project was to really convey the thoughts and emotions one would feel in an abusive relationship. A lot of this was done through the writing. The story was written in second person, so a narrator would walk the player through what was going on in the character's life. In order to help the player get into the mindset of the character, journal entries were added in the first person perspective so they would read thoughts such as "Is this my fault" in the voice of the character and reflect on the choices they have.

    As an editor, I made sure that these point-of-views were kept consistent throughout the story, in order to ensure that the player was immersed. Along with that, I mapped out the different branches and storylines to check whether details, such as a character moving out of an apartment, lined up through each of the story paths. Lastly, I also edited for language fitting the character. As our clients work in the field, they sometimes use language that sounds more clinical than a layman. A small change from "I didn't consent to this" to "I didn't want this" helps reflect the language of a 17-year old who hasn't learned about consent yet.

Art and Animation

Along with the writing, much of the story was conveyed through the art. This included props, characters and backgrounds to illustrate the story. My role included breaking down the story to outline and create descriptions of these art assets, and also create them in Photoshop.

  • Backgrounds: To create backgrounds, I found a reference image, added any elements that needed to be in the scene and modified the lighting to match the time of day. This process is shown to the right.

  • Characters: To create characters, I used metahuman to get base characters. I screenshotted several facial expressions, and collaged images to create the facial expressions I needed. These were then used as reference for the drawings. Several facial expressions of one character are shown to the right.

  • Props: While many of the props were items like a coffe cup or a remote, one of the most powerful ones to tell the story was a phone. Through different phone screens, with details such as Instagram posts with captions and texting patterns, a lot of the story was visualized and put into a modern context.

  • Rotoscoping: To tie into the theme of "In Their Shoes" we wanted to depict a player putting on a character's shoe. To create this animation, I recorded a video of someone putting on a shoe suiting the character, picked out keyframes and drew them to animate it. One of these animations is shown to the right.

Website Design & Development

The last part of the project I worked on was the website. I used a template but modified it to have all of the elements I wanted like a gallery the user could click into to access the games, and use a color scheme that fits the theme of the game and VPVA.
