Voorhees Mall: XR Campus Tour

An Extended Reality experience designed for The Society for College and University Planning conference at Rutgers University, New Brunswick, March 16, 2020.

Roles: Photographer, Designer, Lead Developer

Design & Development


    This project was designed to be a showcase for the possibilities of Augmented Reality technology. To tie into the topic of the conference, we wanted the objects we showcased to be relevant to college planning. Some of our goals and how we met them are outlined below.

  • Create a unique way to experience the buildings on campus.
    The app served as a self-guided tour with game elements. Players take a role in which they are helping people find buildings on the campus. As they help these people, they themselves get acquainted with the campus. With 7 people to help, and an augmented reality map on which buildings show up as they are discovered, the game engages the player to complete the experience and explore the campus.

  • Demonstrate various uses of augmented reality technology.
    With 10 scannable targets, each had a unique 3D model or interaction. While some targets had a 3D model, image or video that represented them, others had mini-games, like a maze, a puzzle and a cannon the player could fire. Along with the signs created for each building, some targets from environmental elements were included. Read more in the Vuforia section.

  • Showcase the rich history of the buildings at Voorhees Mall.
    After researching all of the buildings in Voorhees Mall to find out more about their history, fun facts, flavortext and 3D models were created to showcase their past and present uses.

Vuforia

One of the most interesting things for me with this project was photographing the buildings. The original idea for the AR technology was to use prexisting targets, so the buildings themselves. Environmental factors like the lighting, weather, and seasons, however, would change how the building looked to the AR camera. With limited time to create targets we decided to use photographs of the buildings, and make signs in front of them for the actual event.

In order to capture good augmented reality targets, each photograph had to have unique contrast points as shown below. Since many buildings shared a similar brick facade, I used different angles, and focus points to capture unique and interesting images. Along with this, I wanted atleast one target that wasn't placed by us. Walking around the area I noticed the Rutgers Shield on both banners and benches. The shield being both simple and unique proved to be a great target, and the AR tracking even worked on a stained glass version that wasn't in the database.
