Voorhees Mall: XR Campus Tour

An Extended Reality experience designed for The Society for College and University Planning conference at Rutgers University, New Brunswick, March 16, 2020.

Roles: Lead Developer, Designer, Photographer

Project Goals

Create a unique way to experience the buildings on campus.

Rather than a normal walking tour, the app served as a self-guided experience with game elements. Players take a role in which they are helping people find the resources to accomplish their goals. With 7 people to help, and an augmented reality map on which buildings show up as they are discovered, the game engages the player to complete the experience and explore the campus.

Demonstrate various uses of augmented reality technology.

With 10 scannable targets, each had a unique 3D model or interaction. While some targets had a 3D model, image or video that represented them, others had mini-games, like a maze, a puzzle and a cannon the player could fire. Along with the signs created for each building, some targets from environmental elements were included. Read more in the technology section.

Showcase the rich history of the buildings at Voorhees Mall.

After researching all of the buildings in Voorhees Mall to find out more about their history, fun facts, flavortext and 3D models were created to showcase their past and present uses.
