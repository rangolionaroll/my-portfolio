Rutgers Online Learning Conference Games

This project consists of 2 games designed for the Rutgers Online Learning Conference in 2019 and 2020.

Roles: Designer, Developer

2019

    This project was designed for conference attendees at the 2019 Rutgers Online Learning Conference. The theme of the conference was Education eXtended, so we designed the game to showcase extended reality. We had a virtual reality, an augmented reality, and a real life activity for the conference attendees to complete in order to enter a raffle. They could also use the conference's mobile app to take pictures and submit them to a photo gallery for an extra raffle entry.

  • For our VR activity, we set up a 360 camera inside of a photobooth in which attendees could take a picture. We also set up these images to be seen on an Oculus Go.
  • For our AR activity, I built a simple Vuforia set-up in Unity, in which attendees could hold up the game flyer and a die and particle system would appear. They could move the flyer around to look at the different sides of the die and figure out which side was hidden.
  • For our real life activity, we created conference cards based on the "Sneaky Cards" game. We added prompts such as "create a collaborative doodle" that would get the players to pass on the cards to one another to promote networking. Each attendee started out with two cards.

    I tabled this event for the duration of the conference and interacted with many of the attendees who were curious about the game. The largest thing that I had noticed was that because the prizes (Oculus Go, 360 camera) were really cool, people wanted to enter the raffle, but they weren't as enthusiastic about playing the entire game. Many people did the AR activity because it was quick. Fewer people did the VR photobooth and even less people wanted to use the conference card game. Many came to the table and asked if they could do the activity with us instead of talking to other conference attendees. We got them to form a group to do the activity and talk to each other. What I noticed was that not many people wanted to go out of the way to do extra activities. They were there for the workshops and certainly focused on those. I took this to the team for the 2020 conference game which you can read about below.

2020

    Keeping this in mind, we planned for the 2020 conference. The theme for the conference was "2020 Vision A View to the Future." As part of our presentation at the conference we talked about the game design process and an augmented reality app that we had been working on. As that app had been recieved well, we intended on showcasing augmented reality in the conference app as well. We decided on a collection mechanic for the game in which attendees could scan different things and add objects to their "virtual classroom." To build on the story all of these objects would be tools for teaching and learning.

    Taking the feedback from last year, we knew that the game would have to be incorporated into the main conference app and activties so we outlined the things we would want to feature and promote. This included vendors, workshops and networking. We would give vendors an AR target that people could scan by talking to them, have an AR target at the end of each workshop session, and include a small variety of AR targets on attendee badges. In order to collect them all, attendees could ask if anyone had the object they were missing, and hopefully start a conversation. By merely attending the conference and participating in the conference activities, users could earn raffle tickets towards a prize. Keeping in mind the 2020 vision theme, we decided to have 3 groups of tools as well, the past, present and future. I proposed that the story would follow attendees who all had tools from the past, eg. pencil, quill, typewriter. They would talk to the vendors and collect tools from the present which is what the vendors offered. Lastly, by attending the workshops they would get a glimpse into the future and collect future tools.
