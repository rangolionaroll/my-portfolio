Virtual Therapy Office

This project is a virtual worlds environment designed for the Rutgers School of Social Work.

Roles: 3D Artist, Designer, Developer

Design & Development


This project was designed as a roleplaying environment for students in the Rutgers School of Social Work. Using the office and the waiting room, the students would be able to immerse themselves in the virtual environment and practice their sessions. This project has gone through many iterations, 2 of which I worked on.

The first version created previously at GRID, using Unity is shown below.


Iteration 1

    Using my experience with 3D modeling and Unity I was asked to work on a few things including:
  • Get rid of the clipping planes.
  • Make the environment outside more interesting.
  • Adjust the office and the waiting room to be warmer spaces.
    To redesign this space, I looked at many different therapy office images as well as kept some of the objects from the original project.

    For the technology behind this project, I used Unity to build the world and add the player controller. I used Autodesk Maya and Mudbox to create 3D models, textures, and to animate the objects.

  • Get rid of the clipping planes.
    The original project was created using different primitives that overlapped and caused clipping as the player walked around. When remaking the structure, I used Unity's ProBuilder. With this I was able to customize more complicated shapes within the same object to prevent clipping.

  • Make the environment outside more interesting.
    The original project had a more barren landscape, and buses that were stationary on the road. When redoing the environment, I used Unity's terrain builder, and skyboxes to add nature. Sticking to the original, I also added a road with a bus, but animated a loop in which it drives past the building.

  • Adjust the interior to be warmer spaces.
    I decided to go with yellow walls and add a warm color palette. I also added many objects to make the space feel more personal, some of which include stuffed animals (modeled after our office dogs' toys) next to the couch, a tissue box on the coffee table and children's artwork on the shelf in the office and an animated ceiling fan to add a calm ambiance.

Iteration 2

The most recent version as shown in the trailer above, was created after we shifted our virtual worlds to Mozilla Hubs which is a browser based virtual world, which lets users create and publish their own virtual worlds.

    To bring the therapy office over to Mozilla Hubs, I had to reduce the amount of objects and textures to make sure it would run on all devices. The format of the 3D models also had to be .glb to be compatible, so I used Blender.

  • I created the building in Blender and exported it to Mozilla Hubs. Due to the limitations on the number of objects, I decided to change the environment to be a single photosphere. After trying a few realistic images, I took a 360 image within the Unity build so that I could keep the atmosphere I had built in that version.

  • With the objects inside of the building, I used many of the original objects and lowered their polygon count in Blender. Keeping some of the furniture, I remodeled the chairs since the originals were very high in polygon count. Many decorative objects were deleted, while the most prominent ones were kept. To fit this new world better, I added some features like a painting by Tejaswini, a warm rug and a bulletin board linking to our other worlds.

    • While the first iteration had a lot of small touches, the new iteration was built to be a template that could be customized by the users. They could add objects such as the stuffed animals to their liking. Our team has since used and customized the Therapy Office many times for our virtual meetings.
